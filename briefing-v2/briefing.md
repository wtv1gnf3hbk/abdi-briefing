Islamic State has claimed responsibility for attacks on Syrian government forces in Deir Ezzor province and declared a "new phase" of operations against Damascus, according to [Enab Baladi](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/islamic-state-claims-two-attacks-after-rallying-supporters-in-syria/). The renewed ISIS activity poses a significant challenge to Syria's transitional government as it works to consolidate control over territory previously held by Assad. The group had maintained relative quiet for months as Syria underwent its dramatic political transition, but the escalation suggests ISIS sees opportunity in the ongoing instability as Damascus struggles to integrate various armed factions and establish authority across the country.

**Syria**

• Syria's Interior Ministry established new procedures allowing Kurdish residents to [apply for](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/the-syrian-interior-ministry-sets-procedures-for-syrian-citizenship-applications-for-kurdish-residents/) Syrian citizenship, part of broader efforts to integrate Kurdish-majority areas into the new state structure, Enab Baladi reports. The move follows ongoing negotiations between Damascus and the Syrian Democratic Forces over administrative control of northeastern Syria.

• The Syrian Democratic Forces [released 51 detainees](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/sdf-releases-51-detainees-in-al-hasakah-calls-for-a-phase-of-forgiveness-and-rebuilding/) in al-Hasakah and called for a "phase of forgiveness and rebuilding," according to Enab Baladi. The gesture comes as the SDF negotiates its role in the post-Assad political order.

• More than 1,200 Iraqi nationals remain [unwilling to leave](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/1200-iraqis-unwilling-to-leave-al-hol-camp/) al-Hol camp in Syria, even as the UN [coordinated the return](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/un-coordinates-return-of-191-iraqis-from-al-hol-camp/) of 191 others, per Enab Baladi. The camp continues to house thousands of ISIS-affiliated families.

**Lebanon**

• Lebanon's Hospital Syndicate issued an [urgent statement](https://olnnews.com/181415/) condemning threats against Salah Ghandour Hospital in the south, calling any attack on medical facilities a violation of international law, OLN News (Lebanon) reports.

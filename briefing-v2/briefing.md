Syrian Internal Security forces [dismantled an ISIS cell](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/internal-security-announces-dismantling-of-islamic-state-cell-in-raqqa/) in Raqqa this week as the new government continues consolidating control across the country. Meanwhile, the Trump administration challenged Syria's improving security situation by asking the US Supreme Court to [strip protections](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/2/26/us-government-asks-supreme-court-to-allow-deportation-of-syrian-migrants?traffic_source=rss) from Syrian migrants under Temporary Protected Status, arguing the country is now safe for deportations, Al Jazeera reports. The legal move comes despite ongoing counterterrorism operations and as Human Rights Watch warned that [8,500 detainees](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/human-rights-watch-fate-of-8500-detainees-in-northeast-syria-camps-remains-unknown/) in northeast Syria camps remain in legal limbo. These developments highlight the complex questions surrounding Syria's transition as the government extends authority while international stakeholders debate the country's stability.

**Syria**

• Syrian government forces completed a [detainee swap](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/suwayda-detainee-swap-between-government-and-national-guard/) with Suwayda's "National Guard" faction after negotiations that had been ongoing for days, Enab Baladi reports.

• The government began [rehabilitating Qamishli International Airport](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/syrian-government-begins-rehabilitation-of-qamishli-international-airport/) in the northeast, according to Enab Baladi.

• Syria's ban on foreign trucks entering the country sparked protests from [Jordanian freight firms](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/jordanian-freight-forwarding-and-customs-clearance-firms-protest-syrias-ban-on-foreign-trucks-entering-the-country/) and prompted Lebanon to propose workarounds to [bypass the restrictions](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/lebanese-proposal-to-bypass-impact-of-syrias-ban-on-foreign-trucks/), per Enab Baladi.

• The IMF reported [recovery](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/imf-signs-of-recovery-in-syrias-economy-financial-reform-program-underway/) in Syria's economy as a financial reform program gets underway, while Damascus announced plans for the first [Syrian Travel Expo](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/first-edition-of-the-syrian-travel-expo-to-be-held-in-damascus/) to promote tourism, Enab Baladi reports.

• Internal Security killed the leader of "Saraya al-Jawad" during [clashes](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/leader-of-saraya-al-jawad-killed-in-clashes-with-internal-security/) as the new government continues operations against armed groups, according to Enab Baladi.

**Lebanon**

• The Lebanese Hospital Syndicate issued an [urgent statement](https://olnnews.com/181415/) condemning threats against Salah Ghandour Hospital in the south via leaflets, calling any attack on medical facilities a violation of international law, OLN News reports.

**Broader Levant**

• Israeli forces [struck Hezbollah military compounds](https://www.timesofisrael.com/idf-strikes-hezbollah-military-compounds-in-eastern-lebanon-teenager-said-killed/) in eastern Lebanon, killing a teenager, according to Times of Israel, in the latest escalation since the November ceasefire.

• US-Iran talks in Vienna [concluded](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/2/26/us-iran-talks-conclude-claims-progress-few-details?traffic_source=rss) with claims of progress but few details, Al Jazeera reports, as technical discussions continue but differences remain on avoiding broader conflict.

• Egyptian security forces launched a [crackdown](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/arrests-and-deportations-as-security-crackdown-targets-syrians-in-egypt/) targeting Syrians with arrests and deportations, per Enab Baladi, as regional governments reassess policies toward Syrian refugees following Damascus's stabilization.

Islamic State militants claimed responsibility for at least two attacks against Syrian government forces, according to [Enab Baladi](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/islamic-state-claims-two-attacks-after-rallying-supporters-in-syria/), marking an escalation in ISIS activity following calls for supporters to mobilize across Syria. The group specifically claimed an attack on government positions in Deir Ezzor, per Enab Baladi reporting, as security concerns mount over the group's ability to exploit transitional vulnerabilities. The uptick comes as Syria's new government works to integrate different armed factions and secure territory previously contested during the Assad years.

**Syria**
• The Syrian Interior Ministry established new procedures for Kurdish residents to apply for Syrian citizenship, according to Enab Baladi, as Damascus works to integrate northeastern populations previously excluded under Assad-era policies.

• A government delegation arrived at Qamishli Airport ahead of its planned reopening, Enab Baladi reports, marking another step in Damascus's efforts to restore state control over Kurdish-administered areas.

• The Syrian Democratic Forces [released 51 detainees](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/sdf-releases-51-detainees-in-al-hasakah-calls-for-a-phase-of-forgiveness-and-rebuilding/) in al-Hasakah while calling for a "phase of forgiveness and rebuilding," per Enab Baladi, as the Kurdish-led group signals willingness to cooperate with Damascus.

• Canada eased Assad-era sanctions on Syria while introducing new measures targeting "new perpetrators," according to Enab Baladi, reflecting international efforts to calibrate pressure on the transitional government.

• The UN coordinated the return of 191 Iraqis from al-Hol camp, Enab Baladi reports, though [1,200 Iraqi nationals](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/1200-iraqis-unwilling-to-leave-al-hol-camp/) remain unwilling to leave the facility.

**Lebanon**

• Kuwaiti charitable leader Shafiqa Al-Mutawa expressed hopes to [visit afflicted areas abroad](https://olnnews.com/181437/) to provide support, highlighting ongoing Gulf interest in regional humanitarian assistance, according to OLN News (Lebanon).

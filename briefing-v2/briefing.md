Thousands of Lebanese refugees are fleeing into Syria as Israeli bombing intensifies across Lebanon, reversing the traditional flow of displacement in the region. The exodus comes as debris from the escalating Israeli-Iranian conflict hit [18 locations](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/03/debris-from-israeli-iranian-escalation-hits-18-locations-in-syria/) across Syria, per Enab Baladi, dragging Damascus deeper into a war it has sought to avoid. Al Jazeera reports that [anti-aircraft fire](https://www.aljazeera.com/video/newsfeed/2026/3/7/anti-aircraft-fire-seen-in-eastern-lebanon-amid-israeli-incursion?traffic_source=rss) was seen in Lebanon's Bekaa Valley overnight as Israeli forces attempted another incursion. The regional conflict is forcing Syria to navigate carefully between its Iranian ally and its desire to stay out of the wider war that has already brought [massive explosions](https://www.aljazeera.com/video/newsfeed/2026/3/7/massive-explosions-fires-from-us-israeli-strikes-on-tehran?traffic_source=rss) to Tehran.

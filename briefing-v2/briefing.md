Israeli forces struck key commanders of Iran's Revolutionary Guards Quds Force in Beirut, Reuters reports, marking the deepest Israeli operations into Lebanon since the wider regional conflict began. The strikes come as the broader US-Israel campaign against Iran intensifies across the region, with attacks now reaching as far as Iraq where the US embassy in Baghdad came under fire.

**Syria**

• Syria's Interior Ministry announced the [arrest of a "terrorist cell"](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/03/syrias-interior-ministry-announces-arrest-of-terrorist-cell-in-tartous/) in the coastal city of Tartous, per Enab Baladi, though no details were provided about the group's alleged activities or affiliations.

**Lebanon**

• Lebanese psychologists are documenting how [war affects mental health](https://olnnews.com/181463/), BBC reports, as professionals work to address trauma from ongoing conflict in both adults and children.

• Mental health experts are tracking the [psychological impact of conflict](https://olnnews.com/181462/) on Lebanese communities, according to Syrian Observer, with particular attention to how children process wartime experiences.

**Broader Levant**

• The [US embassy in Baghdad](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/7/us-embassy-in-baghdad-attacked-as-us-israel-war-on-iran-escalates?traffic_source=rss) came under attack as the US-Israel war on Iran escalates, Al Jazeera reports, with Iraq being drawn into the conflict despite government efforts to stay neutral.

• Israel [struck key commanders](https://www.timesofisrael.com/liveblog-march-08-2026/) of Iran's Revolutionary Guards Quds Force in Beirut, per Syria Direct, as Israeli operations intensify against Iranian targets across the region.

• Saudi Arabia has [warned Iran to stop attacks](https://www.timesofisrael.com/saudi-arabia-has-told-iran-to-stop-attacks-warned-of-possible-retaliation-sources-say/) on Gulf states and threatened possible retaliation, sources tell Reuters, as the kingdom faces Iranian drone and missile strikes.

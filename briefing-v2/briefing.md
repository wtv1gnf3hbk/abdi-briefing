Syria's new government faces mounting security challenges as armed groups resist integration into official structures and humanitarian crises persist across the country. The leader of "Saraya al-Jawad" was killed in clashes with Internal Security forces, according to Enab Baladi, while negotiations are underway for prisoner exchanges with southern factions. Legal experts are also questioning the constitutional framework governing the transition as President al-Sharaa implements new policies.

**Syria**

• The leader of "Saraya al-Jawad" was [killed in clashes](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/leader-of-saraya-al-jawad-killed-in-clashes-with-internal-security/) with Internal Security forces, per Enab Baladi, in what appears to be ongoing efforts by Damascus to eliminate armed groups that have not integrated into official security structures.

• Negotiations are underway for a [prisoner exchange](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/negotiations-underway-for-prisoner-exchange-between-syrian-government-and-suwayda-factions/) between the Syrian government and Suwayda factions, Enab Baladi reports, as authorities seek to resolve tensions with the southern province where protests have continued since Assad's fall.

• Human Rights Watch says the fate of [8,500 detainees](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/human-rights-watch-fate-of-8500-detainees-in-northeast-syria-camps-remains-unknown/) in northeast Syria camps remains unknown, highlighting the humanitarian crisis in SDF-controlled territory.

• Legal experts are questioning whether President al-Sharaa [violated the](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/did-president-al-sharaa-violate-the-constitutional-declaration-by-issuing-a-general-amnesty/) Constitutional Declaration by issuing a general amnesty, according to Enab Baladi, raising concerns about the legal framework governing Syria's transition.

• Jordanian freight companies are protesting Syria's [ban on](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/jordanian-freight-forwarding-and-customs-clearance-firms-protest-syrias-ban-on-foreign-trucks-entering-the-country/) foreign trucks entering the country, Enab Baladi reports, threatening cross-border trade.

**Lebanon**

• The Hospital Syndicate issued an [urgent statement](https://olnnews.com/181415/) condemning threats against Salah Ghandour Hospital in the south, according to OLN News (Lebanon), calling on international organizations to protect healthcare facilities.

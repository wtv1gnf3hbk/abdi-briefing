Syria closed its last ISIS-linked detention camp this weekend, evacuating all remaining residents from al-Hol in northeastern Syria after years of international pressure to address the facility's security risks, [Al Jazeera reports](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/2/22/syria-moves-out-last-residents-of-isil-linked-desert-camp). Official Fadi al-Qassem confirmed that all residents have left the camp, which long housed relatives of alleged ISIS members and had become a flashpoint for extremist recruitment and violence. The closure comes as ISIS has escalated attacks against Syrian government forces, with the group [claiming two recent operations](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/02/islamic-state-claims-two-attacks-after-rallying-supporters-in-syria/) according to Enab Baladi, including strikes in Deir Ezzor province.

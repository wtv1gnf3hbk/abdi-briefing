Syrians marked 15 years since the 2011 uprising that eventually toppled Bashar al-Assad's regime in December 2024, but security challenges continue to plague the country, [according to Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/features/2026/3/15/as-syria-marks-15-years-since-anti-assad-uprising-security-issues-remain?traffic_source=rss). The anniversary celebrations come as Damascus welcomes a UN report urging accountability during the transitional period, with Syrian authorities reaffirming their commitment to investigating massacres along the Syrian coast and in Suwayda, [Enab Baladi reports](https://english.enabbaladi.net/archives/2026/03/damascus-welcomes-un-report-reaffirms-commitment-to-investigations-into-the-syrian-coast-and-suwayda/). The transitional government faces mounting pressure to deliver justice while managing ongoing reconstruction efforts hampered by regional conflict and international sanctions.

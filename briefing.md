Iran is escalating its regional military campaign with unprecedented attacks on Gulf states and shipping lanes, drawing international condemnation and raising fears of broader conflict spillover into Syria and Lebanon. [Al Jazeera reports](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/12/five-vessels-attacked-amid-reports-of-iranian-drone-boats-sea-mines?traffic_source=rss) that five vessels were attacked in Iraqi waters using explosive-laden drone boats and sea mines, while Iranian strikes ignited massive fuel tank fires in Bahrain. The UN Security Council adopted a resolution demanding Iran halt its attacks on Gulf nations, [according to Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/12/un-security-council-adopts-resolution-condemning-irans-attacks-in-the-gulf?traffic_source=rss), with the measure cosponsored by 135 countries. The escalation comes as US intelligence assessments suggest the Iranian regime is not at immediate risk of collapse despite the ongoing war, [per Times of Israel](https://www.timesofisrael.com/multitude-of-us-intel-reports-said-to-show-iranian-regime-not-at-risk-of-falling/).
